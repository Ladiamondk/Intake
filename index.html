Roots Community Code

Roots Community Health Center ("Roots") was started for you and all members of our community who need a place to receive care and Information needed to stay healthy. Roots is a place of genuine healing, and it is important that everyone respects this space, Thank you for viewing and supporting our policies and criteria. For these reasons, here are our expectations for Roots:


Personal Conduct:
  • Roots Community Health Center staff will treat patients and community members with professionalism, courteousness, care and respect.
  • Patients will treat Roots staff and providers with courteousness and respect (without any aggressiveness or hostility, verbal threats or intimidation, profane language, physical violence, sexual harassment or other misconduct).
  • Roots patients are prohibited from coming onto the premises intoxicated from alcohol or any other drugs. They are also prohibited from selling or receiving illicit drugs on or near the premises.
  • Roots Community Health Center staff reserves the right to deem any other behaviors to be in violation of our Community Code.
Appointment Reminders:

In order to keep our schedule efficient and accommodating for all of our patients, we have the following guidelines in place Please understand that when you are late, forget, or cancel your appointment without giving enough notice, we miss the opportunity to fill that appointment in time, and patients on our waiting list miss the opportunity to receive pertinent medical care. We know how easy it is to forget an appointment, so we provide courtesy reminder calls 24 hours in advance.

  • If you're scheduled for a 15 minute follow up and arrive MORE than 5 minutes late, your appointment may be canceled or rescheduled. A $5 late fee will also be applied at your next visit.
  • If you are a no call/no show for 3 consecutive appointments, the Patient Care Coordinator will contact you in efforts to create a plan that supports your continuity of care.

No one will be denied service on the basis of race, color, creed, religion, political beliefs, ethnic origin, gender, sexual orientation, age, or physical ability,

You will not be charged for services or denied services provided at Roofs based on your inability to pay, or your insurance status.

Health Measures: Health Care Connections Services for Formerly Incarcerated (Healthy Measures Program Services)


Healthy Measures Program is for men and women with chronic illnesses like High Blood Pressure, Diabetes, Hepatitis, Asthma and Heart Disease who are returning to the community after incarceration, This program will provide you with one-on-one services to give you a "bridge" to caring for yourself/ your health in the community. Whenever possible, the program begins 1-2 months before your release from custody with services provided inside Santa Rita Jail. Your services continue after release in the community, where we will support your transition into care at Roots Community Health Center in East Oakland.


HealthyMeasures offers you support that will integrate your case management, primary medical care, and mental health services, barrier removal services, as well as linkage to social services in four phases:


Phase One: Pre-release-Transition and short-term goal planning while incarcerated, Roots Transition Manager will work with you to prioritize your needs and develop a transitional plan.


Phase Two: Critical Transition- Roots Transition Managr provides a "Warin Handoff to your Roots Health Navigator who will link you to your health/mental care, support you in your self-management goals, and work with you to assess your barriers.


Phase Three: Transition- Meaningful engagement with your medical care and Barrier Removal Activities


Phase Four: Stabilization- ongoing medical care, barrier removal, and long-term stabilization.


Participation in Healthy Measures services is voluntary. Roots staff will make every effort to enroll you in any and all programs and benefits that you may be entitled to such as Medi-Cal and CalFRESH (food stamps), beginning before your release whenever possible. You will not be charged for services or denied services based on your inability to pay, or your insurance status. Your health insurance will be billed for clinical services. You may request referral to another staff member or provider, and may terminate services at any time, either in person or by writing a letter, No one will be denied service.


Our appointment policies allow us the time to inform our standby guests of any availability, as well as keeping our medical staff's schedules filled, thus better serving everyone.
